From Financial Analysis to AI Product Strategy

From analyzing anomalous patterns in financial data to building AI-driven health screening tools, my career path may seem to jump around, but there's a clear internal logic—I've always been searching for ways to use data and technology to solve real business problems.

My product thinking germinated at Kemba Credit Union as an accountant. When I designed a fraud detection system for this $1.6B financial institution, I realized the most valuable work wasn't simply executing analysis, but understanding business pain points, identifying data opportunities, and translating technical solutions into measurable business value. That project automating manual processes and saving 30% labor costs across 20+ branches gave me my first taste of "product thinking" magic.

The real turning point came at Penn's AI Hackathon. As product manager for the DevMind project, I had 48 hours to transform a vague technical concept into a product solving real pain points for 100+ developers. I gained deep understanding of the complete product development process from user research, requirement definition, technical feasibility assessment to MVP delivery. When our team stood out among 50+ teams to become finalists, I was certain I'd found my true passion.

My unique advantage lies in the convergence of three backgrounds: financial education gave me rigorous business thinking and risk assessment capabilities; computer science master's training allows me to engage in deep technical discussions with engineers and accurately assess development complexity; while my accounting data analysis experience gives me natural sensitivity to data-driven decision making. In the EchoMom project, I was able to quickly identify a $2.8B market opportunity and design a go-to-market strategy targeting 15,000+ OB/GYNs—this is the embodiment of cross-domain integration capabilities.

I'm seeking a product team that values data insights, technological innovation, and business impact. I hope to transform my experience accumulated in financial risk control, AI applications, and agile development into excellent products that help users solve real problems. I believe the best product managers need not only empathy and business acumen, but also the ability to translate complex technology into user value—and this is exactly where I'm most confident I can contribute.
