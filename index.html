EchoMom: AI Healthcare for SMBs

B2B SaaS Healthcare Tech Go-to-Market SMB Strategy

Led product strategy for AI-powered postpartum depression screening platform targeting small and medium healthcare practices. Developed comprehensive go-to-market strategy addressing the unique needs of resource-constrained providers.

Product Development Process

1
Market Research & Opportunity Sizing

Conducted comprehensive analysis of the maternal healthcare market, identifying 10,000+ small practices struggling with mental health screening. Analyzed competitor pricing models and discovered gap in SMB-focused solutions.

2
User Discovery & Pain Point Analysis

Interviewed 50+ OB/GYN providers and practice managers. Key findings: 78% cited time constraints, 65% lacked mental health expertise, 82% worried about HIPAA compliance with digital tools.

3
Product Strategy & Roadmap

Designed tiered pricing model based on patient volume (not fixed enterprise fees). Prioritized features: 3-minute voice analysis, EHR integration API, automated referral system, HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure.

4
Go-to-Market Execution

Created multi-channel GTM strategy: educational webinar series (projected 500+ attendees), ROI calculator tool, partnership with 3 medical associations, targeted content marketing addressing SMB concerns.

Competitive Differentiation

Unlike enterprise solutions requiring $100K+ investments, EchoMom's usage-based pricing starts at $299/month, making it accessible for practices with limited budgets while maintaining 65% gross margins.

Technical Architecture

Collaborated with engineering to design microservices architecture enabling rapid deployment. Voice analysis API processes in <2 seconds, supporting 1000+ concurrent users without performance degradation.

Success Metrics Framework

Defined KPIs: CAC <$2,000, LTV:CAC ratio >3:1, monthly churn <5%, NPS >50. Built analytics dashboard tracking user engagement, screening completion rates, and clinical outcomes.

Stakeholder Management

Aligned cross-functional teams through weekly sprints, maintained product requirements documentation, facilitated design thinking workshops with clinical advisors to ensure medical accuracy.

User Research & Product Discovery

Research Methodology

Designed mixed-methods research combining quantitative surveys (n=100) and qualitative interviews (n=25). Segmented developers by experience level, tech stack, and company size to identify patterns.

Key Insights

70% struggle with code consistency, 45% spend >2 hours/week on code reviews, 60% lack automated quality checks. Junior developers particularly affected, spending 30% more time on refactoring.

Persona Development

Created 3 primary personas: "Scaling Startup Dev" (needs speed), "Enterprise Team Lead" (needs consistency), "Solo Developer" (needs guidance). Each persona mapped to specific feature priorities.

Competitive Analysis

Analyzed 15 competitors including SonarQube, CodeClimate. Identified gap: existing tools focus on detection, not automated fixing. Our opportunity: AI-powered auto-remediation with context awareness.
